Land of Maori

New Zealand is an island nation in the southwestern Pacific Ocean.

It contains two main islands (North and South) and numerous smaller islands.

Because of its remoteness, it was one of the last lands to be settled by humans.

During its long period of isolation, New Zealand developed a distinctive biodiversity of animal, fungal and plant life.

The country's varied topography and its sharp mountain peaks owe much to the tectonic uplift of land and volcanic eruptions.

New Zealand has a mild climate with mean annual temperatures ranging from 10 °C to 16 °C.

Christchurch is the driest city, receiving only 640 mm of rain per year and Wellington is the wettest, receiving almost twice that amount.

Auckland, Wellington and Christchurch all receive a yearly average of more than 2,000 hours of sunshine.

The general snow season is about early June until early October in the South Island. Snowfall is less common on the North Island, although it does occur.
